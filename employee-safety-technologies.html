Employee Safety Technologies

Employee safety is the top priority. Without it, operations cannot continue. It includes technical solutions, organizational measures, and strict rules for the operation of equipment. Employee safety is a key priority in modern industrial operations. In mining and construction environments, where risks are significantly higher, advanced safety technologies are an essential part of daily work. Epiroc applies a comprehensive safety approach that combines engineering design, automation, and preventive protection systems to minimize risks and protect personnel in extreme working conditions.

Integrated Safety Systems

Modern Epiroc equipment is equipped with intelligent systems that continuously monitor machine status and working conditions in real time.

These systems include:

  • real-time monitoring of critical machine components
  • automatic detection of faults and anomalies
  • operator warning systems for potential hazards
  • overload and abnormal operation detection

Such technologies help identify risks at an early stage, before they develop into critical failures or accidents.

FOPS Protection System

FOPS (Falling Object Protective Structure) is a structural safety system designed to protect the operator inside the cabin.

Its main purpose is to protect against falling objects in mining and construction environments, such as:

  • rocks and debris
  • structural fragments
  • tools or loose equipment components

The operator’s cabin is engineered to withstand high-impact forces, using reinforced structures and tested high-strength materials.

FOPS is a passive safety system that ensures the operator’s survival space remains protected even under extreme conditions.

DROPS Prevention System

DROPS (Dropped Objects Prevention Scheme) is a set of preventive measures designed to eliminate the risk of objects falling from equipment or structures.

Unlike FOPS, which protects the operator, DROPS focuses on preventing hazards before they occur.

Key measures include:

  • safety tethering of tools and components
  • secure fastening and locking systems
  • vibration-resistant mounting solutions
  • regular equipment inspections
  • strict control of elevated components

DROPS is especially important in underground and multi-level working environments such as mines.

Emergency Safety Systems

Epiroc machines are equipped with advanced emergency response systems designed for immediate action in critical situations.

These include:

  • automatic machine shutdown systems
  • emergency stop functions
  • safety interlock mechanisms
  • automatic machine shutdown systems

These systems ensure rapid response and help prevent escalation of dangerous events.

Workforce Safety Support

In addition to technical solutions, operational safety is supported through structured workforce protection measures.

These include:

  • mandatory safety training programs
  • regular safety briefings and inductions
  • use of personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • strict adherence to safety procedures

Every employee is trained to understand risks and respond correctly in potentially hazardous situations.

Safety Philosophy

The core principle of Epiroc is that safety is not an additional feature — it is an integral part of every technology and process.

Modern safety engineering focuses not only on controlling risks, but on preventing them through design, automation, and intelligent systems.

Conclusion

Employee Safety Technologies at Epiroc represent a fully integrated safety ecosystem that includes:

  • intelligent monitoring systems
  • structural protection (FOPS)
  • preventive safety measures (DROPS)
  • training and operational procedures

Every employee is trained to understand risks and respond correctly in potentially hazardous situations.
